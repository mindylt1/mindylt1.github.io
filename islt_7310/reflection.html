Teaching Philosophy

Mindy Thompson
Advanced Academics Resource Teacher
Department of Defense Education Activity

Teaching is, to me, unlike any other profession. To teach, after all, is to commit yourself again and again--not to profits, products, or to the boss who signs your paycheck, but to a random collection of students for whom you become, at least for the better part of a year, “their” teacher. It is these personal, long-term relationships that make teaching not only unique, but also such an immense responsibility and so highly rewarding.

Unfortunately, this is not something I understood very well when I first began my career in education. I chose teaching because I’d always loved school as a student and because the hours seemed great for me as a mom with young children. Later, during my first few years as a teacher, I found myself buried under so much planning, grading, and paperwork, I struggled to find any joy at all in my students. At the time, teaching seemed to be all about mastering the curriculum, returning graded papers in a timely fashion, keeping up with parent emails, and trying to accomplish that unattainable goal of “catching up” on my work. Many times, I was quite sure I’d made a terrible mistake.

Luckily, through a combination of personal experience and the wisdom of wonderful colleagues, I eventually began to develop a different perspective. While I know that the majority of the elements in my job description refer to things like standards, assessment, and delivering instruction, I also know that successful learning is built on a foundation of feeling safe, respected, supported, and loved. My priorities have now become nurturing relationships and finding the everyday joy in the teaching and learning that happens in my classroom. Not only does the required work still get done and the student learning still happens, but both my students and I also have fun and enjoy one another in the process. Now in my 11th year as a certified teacher, I feel like I’ve found a professional balance and discovered the key to loving what I do.
